Is CapCut Banned? What Happened and What to Use Instead

Updated April 2026 · 4 min read

If you've been following the CapCut situation, you know it's been a rollercoaster. CapCut is owned by ByteDance, the same Chinese company behind TikTok. That connection has put it directly in the crosshairs of U.S. legislation targeting foreign-adversary-controlled applications.

What actually happened

In January 2025, CapCut was pulled from the U.S. App Store alongside TikTok as part of the enforcement of a federal ban on ByteDance-owned apps. It came back shortly after. Then in early 2026, Apple blocked ByteDance apps again. Then it returned again. The pattern has been: ban, restore, ban, restore; with no clear resolution in sight.

The core issue is that CapCut was not included in TikTok's divestiture deal. TikTok itself was transferred to a new U.S. joint venture, but CapCut and other ByteDance products remain fully owned by ByteDance. That means every time enforcement tightens, CapCut is vulnerable.

Can you still download CapCut right now?

As of this writing, CapCut appears to be available on the U.S. App Store. But that could change at any time. If you're building your creative workflow around an app that might disappear tomorrow, that's a real risk. Projects you're in the middle of, effects you've learned, templates you've built; all of that goes away if the app gets pulled again.

The bigger problem beyond the ban

Even when CapCut is available, there are other reasons creators have been looking for alternatives. CapCut moved to a $20/month subscription model, locking features behind CapCut Pro that used to be free. It uploads your video footage to cloud servers for processing. And ByteDance's terms of service grant them broad rights over content created with their tools.
