The Best Free Video Editor With No Watermark in 2026

Most "free" video editors make money by plastering their logo on your exports. It's a frustrating bait-and-switch: you spend an hour editing, hit export, and discover a watermark you can only remove by paying. Here are the editors that are genuinely free with no watermark - and one that stands above the rest.

ByteWave is a relatively new editor that runs entirely on Apple Silicon devices (iPhone, iPad, Mac). It's completely free with no watermarks, no ads, and no subscription tier. What makes it remarkable isn't just the price - it's the feature set. DaVinci Resolve - Desktop powerhouse

DaVinci Resolve has long been the gold standard for free desktop editing. The free version is genuinely capable, with professional color grading, audio editing, and visual effects. The downside is complexity - the learning curve is steep, and it requires a powerful desktop computer. If you need a mobile option, look elsewhere.

iMovie - The reliable default

Apple's iMovie is free and watermark-free, but it's limited compared to what's available now. Basic cuts, transitions, and titles work fine for simple projects, but there's no advanced slow motion, no unlimited audio tracks, and no custom effects.
