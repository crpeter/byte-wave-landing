How to Make Slow Motion Videos on iPhone for Free (No Watermark)

April 2026 · 4 min read

There are two ways to get slow motion on iPhone. You can shoot in slo-mo mode from the Camera app, which records at 120 or 240 fps. Or you can take any normal video and slow it down after the fact using frame interpolation. The second option is more flexible because it works on footage you've already shot, clips from other apps, or videos other people have sent you.

The catch is that most apps that do this well either charge for it or slap a watermark on your export. Here's how to do it for free.

The problem with just slowing down a video

If you take a 30fps video and slow it to half speed, you only have 15 frames per second. The result looks choppy and stuttery. That's why basic editors like iMovie produce rough slow motion on normal footage; they're just stretching what's already there.

Frame interpolation solves this by generating new frames between the existing ones. The AI analyzes motion between two real frames and creates synthetic in-between frames so the result looks smooth even at very slow speeds. This is what makes the difference between amateur-looking slow motion and the buttery smooth effect you see in professional content.

Tips for better slow motion

Shoot in good lighting. Frame interpolation works best when there's clear contrast and detail for the AI to track. Low-light footage with grain will produce lower-quality interpolated frames.

Avoid extremely fast motion. If something moves across the entire frame in a single frame gap, interpolation has less to work with. Moderate motion at 2x to 4x slow-down gives the smoothest results.

Stack motion blur after slowing down. ByteWave lets you add motion blur on top of the slowed footage, which smooths out any remaining artifacts and gives it a more cinematic feel.
