Does CapCut Upload Your Videos to the Cloud? What You Should Know

Updated April 2026 · 4 min read

When you open CapCut and start a project, your video is uploaded to CapCut's cloud servers. This is how CapCut processes many of its effects and AI features — but it also means your raw footage, including personal moments, private content, and unreleased work, is transmitted to and stored on external servers.

For many casual users, this isn't a concern. But for creators working with sensitive material, businesses creating confidential content, or anyone who simply prefers to keep their personal videos private, it's worth understanding what's happening behind the scenes.

What gets uploaded?

When you use CapCut's cloud-dependent features — including certain AI effects, cloud storage, and cross-device syncing — your video data is sent to external servers. CapCut's parent company ByteDance has faced ongoing scrutiny about data practices, which adds another layer of consideration for privacy-conscious users.

The on-device alternative

Editors like ByteWave take a fundamentally different approach. ByteWave processes everything locally on Apple Silicon — frame interpolation, noise reduction, AI upscaling, voice synthesis, and all effects run on your device's processor. No video data is ever transmitted. Your footage stays on your phone, tablet, or Mac at all times.

This on-device approach has performance benefits too. There's no upload/download wait time. You can edit without an internet connection. And export speeds depend on your hardware, not server load.

What should you consider?

If you're editing personal family videos, client work under NDA, pre-release content, or anything you'd rather not have sitting on a third-party server, consider whether cloud-based processing aligns with your comfort level. On-device editors like ByteWave offer the same creative capabilities without the privacy tradeoff.
